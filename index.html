As a horse trainer, my job is much more than teaching a person how to ride a horse. Talent and skill can only get one so far. It is character that molds a great equestrian. Horses are powerful creatures, but they are highly sensitive. They can feel a person's every movement and emotion when they ride. This is why I prioritize my student's mental well being just as much as their physical. I create a vision board for all of my students and their goals, and it is my job to help them succeed. When my students come to the barn, they know it is a safe place to be themselves without judgment. It is an escape from the world around them. It is important that my students know I am apart of their support system, and that they matter. The horses are their therapy, and I help guide them through their experience.

As a leader, I want to inspire confidence in my students. This will greatly impact their performance as a rider. Horses trust confident riders and confidence takes time. I'm present with my students during every lesson, so they know that they are not alone. I provide both positive and negative feedback, and I reassure them that they are doing a great job. I attack their fear with encouraging and supportive words. This will help shape their mental dialogue from fear to determination, so that when they are in a competition they can perform independently.
